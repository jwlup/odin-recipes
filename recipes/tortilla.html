Spanish Tortilla

Description

A Spanish Tortilla is very different from a Mexican or Tex-Mex Tortilla. Instead of a hard or soft piece of flatbread, it is made mostly from eggs and potatoes. As a result, it contains no flour or grains at all. A Tortilla is usually eaten cold and may contain vegetables, herbs and spices to add flavour. During the French occupation of Spain under Napoleon, according to legend, the derogatory term of a "Frenchmen's Tortilla" was coined describing a simple omelette lacking the extra nutrition and flavour of the potatoes.

Ingredients

  • Potatoes
  • Eggs
  • Spices
  • Herbs

Steps

  1. Slice the potaoes into about 1,5 cm thick slices
  2. Crack the eggs, mix them with spices and herbs
  3. Heat Oil in a pan for which you have a good lid
  4. Fry the potaoes
  5. Add the egg mixture, reduce heat and slowly let it cook under the lid
  6. When there is only a little bit of liquid egg mixture on top of the Tortilla, flip it to fry both sides
